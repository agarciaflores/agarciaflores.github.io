Throughout university, there a several different Calculus courses that one can take. For example, there are different versions of calculus courses taught to students in the life sciences, business, engineering, etc. Our Calculus courses are equivalent to the Calculus with Analytic Geometry courses offered at university. Nonetheless, we do our best to include a variety of problems that can be applied to different versions of the course.

Our Calculus I course will walk you through all of the material typically covered in a semester-long university course. Thus, it is reasonable to expect to finish the course in about 3 to 4 months. Each lesson could theoretically be covered in one (long) sitting. Additionally, at the end of every lesson there is problem set on the lesson's material that should be completed before continuing. Each problem set will have around 6 to 12 problems in total, split into three different sections: easy, moderate, and challenging. An answer key to the problem sets will also be provided, with a step-by-step solution to all of the problems.

Below, you will find the lesson plan for the course. We hope it is manageable and efficient.

Lesson Plan:

  1. Functions and Limits
    • Functions: The Basics
    • Limit of a Function
    • Inifinity as a Limit (Undefined Limits)
  2. Limits
    • Numerically
    • Analytically
    • Infinity
  3. Derivatives
    • Chain Rule
    • Product Rule
    • Quotient Rule
    • Inverse Trig Functions
  4. Related Rates
    • Implicit Differentiation
  5. Summations
    • Area Under Functions
  6. Integration
    • Definite Integrals
    • U-Substitution
    • Extrema
    • First Deriviate Test
    • Second Derivative Test
  7. Optimization
