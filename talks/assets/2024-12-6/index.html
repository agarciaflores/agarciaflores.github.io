Poincaré-Bendixson theorem

Suppose that:

  1. $R$ is a closed, bounded subset of the plane;
  2. $\dot{\boldsymbol{x}} = \boldsymbol{f}\left(\boldsymbol{x}\right)$ is a continuosuly differentiable vector field on a open set containing $R$;
  3. $R$ does not contain any fixed points; and
  4. there exists a trajectory $C$ that is "confined" in $R$.

Then either $C$ is a closed orbit, or it spirals towards a closed orbit as $t\to\infty$. In either case, $R$ contains a closed orbit. [1]
