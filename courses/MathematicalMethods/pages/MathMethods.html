Mathematical Methods in Physics

At Arizona State University, the Mathematical Methods in Physics course was split over two semesters. I found this series of courses incredibly insightful, and I very much enjoyed learning from Professor Cecilia Lunardini. The lesson plan below encompasses the majority of the content covered in these classes, as well as incorporating material from other mathematics courses I completed during my undergraduate studies.

Each topic in the lesson plan corresponds to a specific course in a university's catalog, however I will only address the content relevant to those studying physics. This material is not meant to be a comprehensive guide to studying physics, nor does it cover every topic that may be encountered in the field. These are the subjects that I consider essential for excelling as a physicist. Is a thorough understanding of graph theory essential for someone investigating quantum materials? Well, I'm honestly not quite sure. Is it fun? Without a doubt: yes!
