Throughout university, there a several different Calculus courses that one can take. For example, there are different versions of calculus courses taught to students in the life sciences, business, engineering, etc. Our Calculus courses are equivalent to the Calculus with Analytic Geometry courses offered at university. Nonetheless, we do our best to include a variety of problems that can be applied to different versions of the course.

Our Single Variable Calculus course will walk you through all of the material typically covered in a semester-long university course. Thus, it is reasonable to expect to finish the course in about 3 to 4 months. Each lesson could, theoretically, be covered in one (long) sitting. Additionally, at the end of every lesson there is problem set on the lesson's material which should be completed before continuing. Each problem set will be split into three different sections: easy, moderate, and challenging. An answer key to the problem sets will also be provided, with a step-by-step solution to all of the problems.

Below, you will find the lesson plan for the course. We hope it is manageable, efficient, and rewarding.

Lesson Plan:

  1. Functions, Induction, and Limits
    1. Functions: The Basics
    2. Mathematical Induction
    3. Limit of a Function
    4. Inifinity as a Limit, Undefined Limits, Continuity, and Trigonometric Limits
    5. Evaluating Limits

  2. Derivatives
    1. Derivatives, Rate of Change, Slope, and Velocity
    2. Derivatives of Products, Quotients, and Trigonometric Functions
    3. Chain Rule, Higher Derivatives
    4. Implicit Differentiation, Inverses
    5. Exponential and Logarithmic Functions
    6. Logarithmic Differentiation, Hyperbolic Functions

Problem Sets:

  1. Problem Set 1: Limits (Solutions)
